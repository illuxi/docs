## Product types

There are 2 types of product: synchronous and asynchronous activities.

| Type         | Description |
| ---------    | ----------- |
| Synchronous    | A synchronous product is an activity happening at a specific date and time       |
| Asynchronous | An asynchronous product is typically composed of multiple [capsules](/concepts/capsule)       |

### Synchronous

A synchronous product is an activity happening at a specific date and time.

<Tip>

For example, an Introduction to Microsoft Excel, a 4 hours training set to occur on June 1st.

</Tip>

Synchronous activities can have limited places available, waiting lists, certificates and automatic reminders.

They can also be of different event types to allow different kinds of training configurations.

#### Online

An online event type is an activity happening on a specific date and time, online.

<Tip>

For example, an Introduction to Microsoft Excel, a 4 hours training set to occur on June 1st, on a **Zoom web conference**.

</Tip>

#### In-person

An in-person event type is also an activity happening on a specific date and time, but in-person (like in the good old days 😏).

<Tip>

For example, an Introduction to Microsoft Excel, a 4 hours training set to occur on June 1st, at **the office of ABC inc.** located at 123 ABC Street, Montreal.

</Tip>

#### Hybrid

An hybrid event type is an activity happening simultaneously online and in-person.

<Tip>

For example, an Introduction to Microsoft Excel, a 4 hours training set to occur on June 1st, at **the office of ABC inc.** and via a **Zoom web conference**.

</Tip>

#### Multiple sessions

A multiple sessions event type is an activity broke down into more than one sessions, bound together within a specific group.

For example, let's say the Microsoft Excel Advanced course is a 16 hours traning broke down into 4 sessions of 4 hours each. You can offer users to register to a **fixed set** of these 4 sessions or offer **different sets of sessions**. The user will be able to choose which set of session he choose to attend:

<Tip>

**Option 1:** September 3, 4, 5, from 8 AM to 12PM, on Zoom

**Option 2:** October 10, 11, 12, from 1PM AM to 5PM, on at the office of ABC inc

</Tip>


### Asynchronous

An asynchronous product is typically composed of multiple [capsules](../capsule), each representing an item of content. Capsules can be a video, a PDF, a SCORM, a quiz, etc. A capsule can even be a synchronous activity.
