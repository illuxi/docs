## Product types

There are 2 types of product: synchronous and asynchronous activities.

| Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Synchronous | Activity scheduled for a specific date and time. |
| Asynchronous | Self-paced activity typically made up of multiple capsules. |

### Synchronous

A synchronous product is a training activity that takes place on a scheduled date and time.

<Tip>
  For example, Introduction to Microsoft Excel, a 4-hour training session scheduled for June 1st.
</Tip>

Synchronous activities may include features such as limited capacity, waiting lists, certificates, and automated reminders.

They can also be configured with different **event types** to support various training formats:

#### 1- Online

An online event type is a synchronous activity delivered virtually at a scheduled date and time.

<Tip>
  For example, Introduction to Microsoft Excel,  a 4-hour training session scheduled for June 1st, via **Zoom.**
</Tip>

#### 2- In-person

An in-person event type is a synchronous training activity delivered onsite at a scheduled date and time.

<Tip>
  For example, Introduction to Microsoft Excel, a 4-hour training session scheduled for June 1st, at  **ABC inc's office** located at 123 ABC Street, Montreal.
</Tip>

#### 3- Hybrid

A hybrid event type is a synchronous activity that takes place both online and in person at the same time.

<Tip>
  For example, Introduction to Microsoft Excel could be offered as a 4-hour training session scheduled for June 1st at ABC Inc.’s office and simultaneously via Zoom.
</Tip>

#### 4- Multiple sessions

A multiple sessions event type is a training activity divided into several sessions grouped together within a specific set.

For example, _Microsoft Excel Advanced_ could be offered as a 16-hour training course divided into four 4-hour sessions. Users can register for a **predefined set of sessions** or choose from **multiple available session sets**.

<Tip>
  **Option 1:** September 3, 4, 5, 6 from 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM, via Zoom

  \
  **Option 2:** October 10, 11, 12, 13, from 1:00 PM to 5:00 PM, at ABC Inc.’s office
</Tip>

### Asynchronous

An asynchronous product is typically composed of multiple [**capsules**](capsule), each representing a unit of content.

A capsule can be a video, a PDF, a SCORM package, a quiz, or even a synchronous activity.
